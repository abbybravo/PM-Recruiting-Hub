# 🎯 PM Interview Resources

PM interviews typically fall into four categories. Most processes include a mix of 2–4 of these across multiple rounds.

---

## Product Sense

Tests your ability to design and evaluate products with strong user empathy and judgment.

**Common prompts:**
- "Design a product for [user group]."
- "How would you improve [existing product]?"
- "What's your favorite product and why?"

**Framework to practice:**
1. Clarify the goal and constraints (who, what business context, what success looks like)
2. Identify the target user and their core pain point
3. Brainstorm 2–3 solutions, pick one, and justify the tradeoff
4. Define how you'd measure success
5. Call out risks or edge cases

**Practice tip:** Pick one app you use daily and do this full framework out loud, twice a week.

---

## Execution Interviews

Tests analytical and prioritization skills — often framed as a metrics drop, prioritization scenario, or go-to-market question.

**Common prompts:**
- "Metric X dropped 20% last week — how do you investigate?"
- "You have 3 features and 1 engineer for the quarter — how do you prioritize?"
- "How would you launch [product] in a new market?"

**Framework to practice:**
1. Clarify the metric definition and timeframe
2. Segment the problem (external vs. internal, sudden vs. gradual)
3. Form hypotheses, ranked by likelihood and ease of checking
4. State what data you'd pull to confirm/deny each hypothesis
5. Propose a next step or fix

---

## Behavioral Interviews

Tests communication, self-awareness, and how you've operated under real constraints.

**Common prompts:**
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate."
- "Tell me about a project that failed."
- "Tell me about a time you had to influence without authority."

**Framework to practice:** STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) — but add a **Reflection** line: what you'd do differently, or what you learned. This signals maturity beyond just reciting a win.

**Practice tip:** Prepare 5–6 core stories that can flex across multiple prompt types (leadership, conflict, failure, ambiguity, cross-functional work) rather than memorizing one story per possible question.

---

## Metrics Questions

Tests your fluency in defining and reasoning about product metrics.

**Common prompts:**
- "How would you measure the success of [product/feature]?"
- "What metrics would you track for [product]?"
- "Walk me through a metrics tree for [product]."

**Framework to practice:**
1. Start from the product's core value proposition
2. Define a **North Star metric**
3. Break it into a small metrics tree (acquisition, engagement, retention, monetization as relevant)
4. Pick 2–3 metrics you'd actually watch weekly, and explain why those over others

---

## General Interview Prep Tips

- Practice out loud, not just in your head — timing and verbal clarity matter as much as the answer's content.
- Record yourself once or twice; most people are surprised by their own filler words or pacing.
- Ask 1–2 thoughtful clarifying questions before diving into product sense or execution answers — interviewers notice when candidates skip this.
- Have 2–3 questions ready to ask the interviewer at the end — genuine curiosity about their team goes a long way.
