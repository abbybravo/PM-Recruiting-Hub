# 🤝 Networking Resources

Networking is one of the highest-leverage activities in PM recruiting. Many interview opportunities and referrals come directly from a genuine conversation, not a cold application.

---

## Cold Outreach Templates

Keep messages short, specific, and low-pressure. Avoid generic "can I pick your brain" messages with no context.

**LinkedIn / Email — Initial Outreach:**

> Hi [Name], I'm a [year] studying [major] at [school], and I came across your background in PM at [Company], particularly your work on [specific product/team, if known]. I'm exploring a path into product management and would love to hear about your experience if you have 15 minutes sometime in the next couple of weeks. Happy to work around your schedule. Thanks so much for considering it!

**Follow-up (if no response after ~1 week):**

> Hi [Name], just following up in case my last message got buried, totally understand if you're busy. Would still love to connect if timing works, no pressure either way!

**After a referral or introduction:**

> Hi [Name], [Mutual Contact] suggested I reach out, they mentioned your work on [team/product] and thought it'd be a great conversation for me as I explore PM. Would you be open to a quick chat in the next couple weeks?

---

## Coffee Chat Tips

- **Do your homework first.** Know their current role, past companies, and (if possible) a product they've shipped.
- **Lead with a specific question**, not "tell me about your career." E.g., "How did you decide between staying technical vs. moving into PM?"
- **Keep it to the time you asked for** (usually 15–20 minutes) unless they explicitly offer more.
- **Don't ask for a referral in the first conversation.** Build the relationship first as referrals tend to come naturally in a second conversation or after genuine follow-up.
- **Send a thank-you note within 24 hours**, referencing something specific from the conversation.
- **Follow up later with an update**, not just an ask, e.g., "Wanted to let you know I applied to [Company] and really appreciated our chat, it shaped how I think about [topic]."

---

## Alumni Networking

- Use your school's alumni database or LinkedIn's school filter to find PM alumni, start with people who graduated 2–5 years ago (often most willing to help and most memory of the process).
- Student orgs (SHPE, ColorStack, SWE, PM clubs, business fraternities) often have informal alumni networks, ask club leadership for warm introductions.
- Attend alumni panels and career events even if not PM-specific; many attendees know someone in PM and can make an introduction.
- Keep a simple running list of who you've talked to, what you discussed, and any follow-up you owe them — this prevents awkward "did I already ask you this" moments and helps you nurture the relationship over time.
