# 📅 PM Recruiting Timeline

A general guide to when to prepare, network, and apply for Product Management internships and new grad roles. Timelines vary by company, so always check individual postings — but this gives a realistic default schedule.

---

## Freshman / Sophomore Year: Preparation

**Goal:** Build the foundation before you start applying.

- Join a relevant student org (Product Management club, SWE, SHPE, entrepreneurship clubs, case competition teams)
- Take on a project with real users — a side app, a class project, a hackathon build — anything that produces a story
- Start following PM-focused content (blogs, podcasts, newsletters) to build product vocabulary
- Do informational interviews with upperclassmen or alumni in PM roles — no pitch needed, just learn
- Consider a technical or business internship (even non-PM) to build a track record before targeting PM specifically

---

## Junior Year Summer Prep (May – July, before Fall recruiting)

**Goal:** Get application-ready before postings open.

- Finalize your resume — see [Resume Resources](resume-resources.md)
- Draft a short "why PM" narrative you can adapt for cover letters and interviews
- Start a target company list, split into reach / match / safety
- Begin light mock interview practice on product sense fundamentals
- Set up job-alert tracking (this repo, LinkedIn alerts, company career pages)

---

## Fall Recruiting (August – November)

**Goal:** Apply broadly and efficiently — this is peak volume for internship postings.

- Most APM programs and PM internships for the following summer open in this window
- Apply within the first 1–2 weeks of a posting going live when possible — many companies review on a rolling basis
- Attend company info sessions and career fairs; these often lead directly to referrals
- Track every application (company, date applied, status) — this repo's [status legend](../README.md#-status-legend) can help standardize your own tracker too

---

## Interview Season (September – January)

**Goal:** Convert applications into offers.

- Expect 2–4 rounds: recruiter screen → product sense / execution → behavioral → (sometimes) final panel
- See [Interview Resources](interview-resources.md) for a breakdown by interview type
- Do 1 mock interview per real interview you have scheduled, if possible
- Send a short, genuine thank-you note after each round

---

## Spring Recruiting (January – April)

**Goal:** Catch remaining and off-cycle postings.

- Some companies post additional roles in spring, especially smaller companies and startups
- New grad roles for graduating seniors are often heaviest in this window
- Off-cycle internships (non-summer start dates) are more common in spring postings

---

## Quick Reference

| Timeframe | Primary Focus |
|---|---|
| Freshman / Sophomore | Projects, clubs, informational interviews |
| May – July | Resume, target list, mock interview basics |
| August – November | Bulk applications, career fairs, info sessions |
| September – January | Interview rounds |
| January – April | Remaining postings, new grad recruiting |
